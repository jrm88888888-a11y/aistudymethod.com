Density: Float or Sink?

Archimedes' principle · Upthrust · ρ = m/V

An object floats when its density is less than the fluid's density. Upthrust equals the weight of fluid displaced (Archimedes' principle) — this is why a steel ship floats: its average density (hull + air) is less than water.
