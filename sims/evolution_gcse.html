Evolution by Natural Selection

Variation · Selection · Inheritance · Adaptation over generations

Evolution occurs because: individuals vary in heritable traits; some variants survive and reproduce more successfully; offspring inherit those traits. Over many generations, the population's mean trait shifts towards the favoured end of the distribution.
