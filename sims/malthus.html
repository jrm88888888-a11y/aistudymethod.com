Stages of the DTM

A four-stage canonical model

Stage 1 — Pre-industrial. High BR, high DR. Population stable, slow.
Stage 2 — Death rates fall (sanitation, vaccines). BR still high. Population explodes.
Stage 3 — BR falls (contraception, urbanisation). Growth slows.
Stage 4 — Low BR ≈ low DR. Stable, sometimes shrinking.
