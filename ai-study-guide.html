Tier 1 — Prompt

The foundation of AI-assisted learning. Use targeted prompts to get AI to explain, quiz, diagnose, and guide your study — one interaction at a time. Every prompt maps onto a Velvet Method step.

Single interaction · Topic-specific · Immediate result
V
Map Your Syllabus with AI
Step 1 — View
"I'm studying AQA A-Level Biology. Generate a complete topic map showing how all topics connect and where the most marks are."
"What are the most commonly tested command words in GCSE Chemistry and what does each demand?"
E
Diagnose Your Gaps
Step 2 — Evaluate
"Ask me questions across all AQA A-Level Physics topics to identify my weakest areas. Give me a priority list at the end."
"Quiz me on GCSE Biology Cell Division. Probe deeper where I seem uncertain. Then tell me what to focus on."
L
Study with AI as Your Partner
Step 3 — Learn
"Explain enzyme inhibition using an analogy I've never heard before."
"I don't understand supply and demand shifts. Explain it three different ways until one clicks."
V
Test Your Knowledge
Step 4 — Verify
"Generate 8 AQA A-Level Chemistry exam questions on organic reactions. Mark my answers with examiner feedback."
"Give me a 15-minute timed challenge on GCSE Physics Waves. No hints. Mark me strictly."
E
Apply and Analyse
Step 5 — Explore
"Present me with an unseen data set on enzyme activity. Ask me to analyse it as an A-Level examiner would expect."
"Give me a synoptic question crossing Cell Biology, Genetics, and Ecology at A-Level standard."
T
Reflect and Plan
Step 6 — Transform
"Review our session. Which topics seem secure and which need another cycle? Build me a spaced repetition schedule."
"Evaluate my exam technique based on my answers today. What specific habits should I change?"

Tier 3 — Engineer

The most sophisticated level of AI-assisted learning. Rather than building one resource for one topic, you build a system — a reusable engine that generates unlimited resources for any subject, any topic, any level. This is not prompting. This is architecture.

Reusable system · Topic-agnostic · Generates unlimited instances

The distinction between Tier 2 and Tier 3 is the difference between a craftsman and an engineer. A Tier 2 student asks AI to build a Pac-Man game for French food vocabulary. A Tier 3 student asks AI to build a vocabulary game engine that accepts any word list, any subject, any difficulty level — and generates a Pac-Man game, a Space Invaders game, or a Memory Match game on demand. One system. Unlimited outputs. That is engineering.
